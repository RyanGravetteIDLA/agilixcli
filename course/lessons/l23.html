Super-Admin Self-Recovery & Education Defaults

Whether a locked super admin can recover their own account hinges on a single checkbox — one that ships OFF by default for nearly every K-12 edition. Knowing its state, and why Google recommends leaving it off, is the heart of the security-vs-recoverability trade-off.

The toggle

Find it at Admin console → Menu → Security → Authentication → Account recovery → Super admin account recovery. The exact checkbox reads "Allow super admins to recover their account."

Default state by edition

The setting is OFF by default for these editions:

  • Education Standard
  • Education Plus
  • Business Plus
  • Frontline Standard
  • Enterprise Essentials Plus
  • G Suite Basic
  • Cloud Identity Premium

It is ON by default for other editions. For K-12 districts and online schools, the practical assumption is simple: your edition almost certainly ships with self-recovery OFF.

When the toggle is OFFA locked super admin who clicks "Forgot password?" is told to contact their administrator — there is no self-service path. Recovery must go through another super admin or Google Support (the domain-ownership escalation from L2.2). If you have neither a second admin nor DNS control, an OFF toggle plus a single locked admin is a permanent-lockout scenario.

Why Google recommends keeping it OFF

Google's security guidance treats self-recovery disabled as the more secure posture. The reasoning: a self-recovery channel (the recovery email or phone) is itself an attack surface — phishing or social-engineering that channel can hand an attacker your highest-privilege account. Disabling it removes that surface.

The trade-off is non-negotiable: if self-recovery is off, you must maintain a second super admin or a documented break-glass path. Otherwise you have hardened the front door and bricked the only key.

OFF vs ON, side by side

SettingWhat it buys youWhat it costs you
OFF (Google-recommended) More secure — no recovery-channel attack surface to phish or socially engineer. No self-service recovery. Requires a second super admin or a break-glass path, or you risk permanent lockout.
ON Self-service recovery — a locked admin can recover via pre-set recovery email/phone without a colleague. The recovery channel becomes an attack surface. Phishing or social-engineering the recovery email/phone can compromise a super admin account.
Director takeawayPhone recovery for super admins stays on regardless of this setting — but do not treat that as your safety net. The defensible K-12 posture is the one Google recommends: keep self-recovery OFF, and back it with at least two super admins plus a documented break-glass account. You get the hardened attack surface and a guaranteed way back in. Choosing OFF without that second key is the most common self-inflicted permanent lockout.
Verify in your consoleThe per-edition default list is exactly the kind of detail Google revises without fanfare, and edition names themselves shift over time. Do not assume — open Security → Authentication → Account recovery → Super admin account recovery and confirm the current state of "Allow super admins to recover their account" for your specific edition.
