Recovery Info, DNS & the Registrar Dependency

Per-account recovery info is the easy half; control of your primary domain's DNS is the dependency that decides whether automated recovery is even possible.

Recovery info on admin accounts

Every admin account should carry two recovery channels, each independently reachable:

  • Recovery email — an address separately accessible and different from the Workspace address. If the Workspace account is locked, you must still be able to read this inbox.
  • Recovery phone — a mobile that receives SMS, belonging to that specific admin.

Set it per user at:

Admin console → Directory → Users → [user] → Security → Recovery information → Save

Org-level recovery-info controls

Added December 2024, two settings govern whether users may add recovery email and recovery phone, scoped by OU or group:

Security → Account recovery → Recovery information

  • These controls do not apply under third-party SSO or password sync — in those configurations recovery flows through your external IdP, not Google.
  • Super admins: recovery phone is always enabled for super admins regardless of these settings.

The deeper dependency: DNS proves you own the organization

This is the part most districts underestimate. Google treats DNS control of your primary domain as proof of organizational ownership. When self-service options are exhausted, the automated super-admin recovery escalation asks you to add a CNAME or TXT record to the primary domain's DNS to prove you control it.

If you have lost DNS access, that path fails entirely. Common ways districts lose it:

  • The domain is managed by a vendor or contractor who has since departed.
  • The registration quietly expired because billing was tied to one person.
  • Nobody knows the registrar or the login to the DNS zone.

Lost DNS is one of the top causes of permanent lockout, because it removes the recovery mechanism of last resort.

How districts get permanently locked outIf you cannot add a CNAME/TXT record to your primary domain — departed vendor, expired registration, unknown registrar login — Google's automated ownership-based recovery is blocked entirely. A locked tenant whose domain you can no longer prove you own may be unrecoverable.

What to do now

  • Inventory and secure registrar + DNS-zone credentials offline. Know the registrar, the account login, and who can reach the DNS zone today.
  • Document who controls them and ensure domain ownership and billing are not tied to a single person who could depart.
  • Do not tie the only admin's recovery to a personal phone or email. Compromising that one channel hands over your most powerful account.
  • Remove recovery info from departed or compromised admins immediately — stale recovery channels are a standing back door.
Verify in your consoleThe org-level account-recovery settings and their labels are recent (Dec 2024) and behave differently under SSO/password sync. Confirm current behavior at Security → Account recovery, and verify the per-user path under Directory → Users still matches your console — Google's labels drift.
Director takeawaySet independent recovery email and phone on every admin, then treat registrar and DNS-zone access as tier-one recovery infrastructure: inventory it, store the credentials offline, and make sure no single departing person can take your domain — and your only recovery path — with them.
