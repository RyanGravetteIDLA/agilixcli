Enforcement Without Lockouts

Allowing 2SV is harmless. Enforcing it is where districts lock people out. The difference between a clean rollout and a help-desk flood is sequencing, grace periods, and a recovery net staged before the cutover.

The enforcement dropdown

Under Security → Authentication → 2-Step Verification, the enforcement control offers three states:

SettingBehavior
Off2SV is voluntary. Users may enroll, but sign-in does not require it.
OnEnforced immediately. Anyone not enrolled is blocked at next sign-in.
Turn on enforcement from dateDate picker sets a future cutover. The change typically takes effect within ~24–48 hours of the chosen date.

The new-user enrollment grace period

Pair enforcement with a New user enrollment period. This grace window lets brand-new users sign in with password only while they get their second factor set up. Configurable values run from 1 day to 6 months (1 day, 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, 6 months). Without it, a newly created account in an enforcing OU is locked out before it can enroll.

How people actually get locked out

Three failure modes account for most enforcement-day lockouts:

  • A user is moved into an enforcing OU while still unenrolled.
  • A stricter method policy is enforced (e.g., "Only security key") without the user holding the required factor.
  • A user removes their last second step and has no fallback.

The safe rollout pattern

  1. Notify users with dates, the "why," and enrollment instructions.
  2. Allow 2SV with Enforcement Off so users can enroll on their own schedule.
  3. Drive voluntary enrollment through reminders and deadlines.
  4. Track enrollment using the Admin console security reports to see who is and isn't covered.
  5. Then enforce — phasing by OU or by configuration group so problems stay contained.

Precedence: use a configuration group as a holding pen

Remember the precedence rule: "Group settings override organizational units." You can exploit this. Create a configuration group of not-yet-ready users with enforcement OFF, let them enroll inside it, then move them into the enforcing OU once they hold a second factor. The group override shields stragglers from the OU's enforcement until they're ready — no premature lockout.

Education-specific: Google enforces 2SV on admins for youFor Workspace for Education, Google now mandates 2SV on admin accounts as its own policy, separate from anything you configure. Super admins receive ~90 days notice; other admins ~60. After the date, lockout is progressive: reminders, then mobile access blocked at 15 days, then web access blocked at 30 days. Critically: "Admins subject to the Google-set 2SV enforcement policy cannot bypass it" — the only alternative is removing the admin role. Enroll every super admin now and stage their backup codes (L4.4).
Verify in your consoleConfirm the current enrollment-period increments and the exact enforcement labels at Security → Authentication → 2-Step Verification, and check your live enrollment coverage in the security reports before setting any cutover date. Google's defaults and effective-time windows in this area drift.
Director takeawayNever flip enforcement "On" cold. Allow → drive enrollment → verify coverage → enforce by OU or group, with a configuration-group holding pen for stragglers and backup codes pre-staged. The Google-set admin mandate means your super admins are on a clock whether or not you act — treat that as the first deadline.
